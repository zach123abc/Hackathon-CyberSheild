Scam Awareness

Phishing scams trick you into giving away personal info. Here are red flags to watch for:

Common Signs of a Scam Email

  • Urgent or threatening language (“Your account will be closed today!”).
  • Unfamiliar or suspicious sender addresses.
  • Links that look odd when you hover over them.
  • Requests for passwords or personal info.
  • Poor spelling or formatting.

Example of a Phishing Email

“Dear User,
Your account has been compromised. Please click here to verify your identity.”

How to Protect Yourself

  • Check the sender’s email domain carefully.
  • Never click unknown links — go to the official site manually.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) where possible.
  • Report suspicious messages to your provider or IT support.
